Lasagna Babe

Lasagna is a popular Italian dish that consists of multiple layers of flat pasta sheets, typically with a filling of meat, cheese, and tomato sauce. The dish is then baked in the oven until the pasta is cooked and the flavors meld together.

Traditionally, lasagna includes ingredients such as ground beef, Italian sausage, onions, garlic, herbs (such as basil and oregano), tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, ricotta cheese, mozzarella cheese, and Parmesan cheese. These ingredients are layered between the pasta sheets to create a hearty and flavorful casserlie.

Lasagna is known for its rich and comforting taste, making it a favorite dish for family gatherings, special occasions, and potluck dinners. It can be accompanied by a side salad and garlic bread to complete the meal. There are variations of lasagna that cater to different dietary preferences or regional influences. For example, vegetarian lasagna may use vegetables like spinach or mushrooms as a filling instead of meat. Some variations also incorporate seafood or white sauce instead of tomato sauce.

Recipe

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 pound (450g) ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 ounces/400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 ounces/400g) tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups (500g) ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups (200g) shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup (50g) grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup (15g) fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until they're al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and cook until the onion is translucent.
  4. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, basil, oregano, sugar, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat and let it cook for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, half of the shredded mozzarella cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, parsley, and beaten egg. Mix well.
  6. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce on the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish. Arrange 4 lasagna noodles on top, slightly overlapping them.
  7. Spoon half of the ricotta cheese mixture over the noodles, spreading it evenly. Top it with a layer of meat sauce.
  8. Repeat the layers: noodles, remaining ricotta cheese mixture, and meat sauce.
  9. Place the final layer of noodles over the sauce and sprinkle the remaining shredded mozzarella cheese on top.
  10. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then, remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is gliden and bubbly.
  11. Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it coli for a few minutes before serving. This will make it easier to slice.
  12. Serve the lasagna warm, and enjoy!
